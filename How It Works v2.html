How it works

From your first question to closing day

Before you create an account

01

Run the calculators

Start with the closing cost calculator, the affordability calculator, or the mortgage comparison tool. These are free, no login required, and built specifically for NJ and NY transactions. You'll see real numbers: title insurance, transfer taxes, attorney fees, lender charges — broken down line by line. Most buyers don't see this level of detail until they're already under contract. You can start here.

No account needed
02

Take the assessment

Eight questions, about two minutes. Have you spoken to a lender? Do you have an agent? What price range are you considering? There's no wrong answer and no pressure to be further along than you are. The assessment is how Closing Map figures out what to show you first and what can wait. At the end, you're invited to create an account and save your results.

Takes about 2 minutes

After you create your account

03

Get your readiness score and dashboard

After the assessment, your personalized ClosingMap dashboard is generated. Your readiness score reflects how prepared you are to move when the right property comes along. Your journey tracker shows every stage between now and closing day. Your next recommended action is surfaced prominently. As you make progress, your score advances. Nothing resets.

04

Build your profile

Your profile is made up of five components: a team (your agent, loan officer, and closing attorney), a document vault (government ID, financial records, pre-approval letter, and agency disclosures), a properties watchlist, a showings calendar, and a financing track. The assessment pre-fills what the system already knows from your answers. It flags what's still missing and tells you what to gather next. You're never starting from a blank form.

05

Track properties on your watchlist

Add any property to your watchlist by pasting a link or entering an address. Your agent can also add properties directly. Each property has its own card showing address, price, and current status — from "browsing" through "offer submitted." You can track as many as you want. Nothing becomes a transaction until an offer is submitted and accepted.

06

Connect with professionals when you're ready

Once your profile is built out, Closing Map surfaces recommended agents, lenders, and attorneys tailored to your budget, location, timeline, and property type. You decide who you want to talk to. Nobody buys your contact information. Nobody is paying to appear on your list.

You choose. No pressure.
07

Offer accepted. Your preparation becomes the deal.

When an offer is submitted and accepted, your watchlist property transitions into an active transaction. The deal sheet generates automatically. Your contract is drafted and sent for signatures. Once signed, the coordination work begins across your full team: agent, lender, attorney, and title. Your profile, your team, your documents, and your preparation all carry forward. You don't re-enter anything.

The finish line

Before you create an account

01

Start with the net sheet

Before anything else, run the seller net sheet. Enter your asking price, your mortgage payoff balance, and your location. The calculator returns your projected proceeds after commissions, closing costs, transfer taxes, and payoff. Most sellers don't find out their real net until they're sitting at the closing table. You can find out now, free, no account required.

No account needed
02

Take the assessment

Six questions about where you are in the process. Are you selling immediately or still planning? Do you have an agent? What steps have you already taken? The assessment is short and meets you wherever you are. It also asks whether you'd like to make your listing more competitive — that's where the Verified Seller Program gets introduced.

Takes about 2 minutes

After you create your account

03

Get your readiness score and dashboard

Your seller dashboard shows your readiness score and the full path ahead: listing strategy, inspection, title and escrow, closing preparation, and closing day. Your next recommended step is surfaced prominently. As you complete steps — gathering documents, connecting with your agent, completing title work — your score advances.

04

Build your seller profile

Your document vault collects the papers sellers need at closing: the deed, current property tax bills, existing mortgage information, your prior title policy, any existing survey, a capital improvements list, and utility records. Your team includes your listing agent, your closing attorney, and — if applicable — a title company. The system tells you what's complete and what's still outstanding.

05

Consider the Verified Seller Program

Most title issues surface one to two weeks before closing — when there's no time and no good options. Closing Map's Verified Seller Program runs a preliminary title search before you list. If there's a lien, an outstanding judgment, or a gap in the chain of title, you'll know while you still have time to fix it. Sellers who complete the process receive a Verified Seller badge that signals transaction-readiness to buyers and their agents. Problems found before listing are far easier to resolve than problems found the week before closing.

Strongly recommended
06

List, accept, and hand off

Once your property is listed and an offer comes in, you review and accept it from your dashboard. At that point, the deal moves forward — contract drafting, signatures, and the coordination work that takes a transaction from signed contract to closing day. Your documents carry forward. Your team carries forward. You don't restart from scratch.

When a deal is under contract

The work you did here goes with you

When a property enters an active transaction — offer accepted, contract signed — the coordination work begins across your full team. Your attorney, your agent, your lender, your title professional. That process runs through inspection, title work, financing, and closing day.

What you built in Closing Map goes with it. The agent you chose is the agent running your deal. The pre-approval you uploaded is the one your attorney sees. The disclosures you already signed are already signed. Nobody restarts from zero. The preparation you did here is the preparation everyone else works from.
